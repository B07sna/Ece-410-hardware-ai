# Critical Path Analysis — M3 top Module

The critical register-to-register path begins at the **DFFR Q output of bit 1 of the weight-accumulation input register** inside `compute_core`'s pipeline Stage 0 (the parallel-multiplier bank), passes through **21 combinational logic stages** mapped by ABC across the generic_130nm library, and terminates at the **D input of the output-stage pipeline flip-flop** that captures the quantised INT8 result in Stage 4. The 21-stage chain traverses, in order: a weight-gating MUX2 (selecting between zero and the signed partial product), three levels of XNOR2/AOI21/OAI21 carry-chain cells implementing the ripple-carry addition of the four-level adder tree (Stages 1–3 of the pipeline collapsed into a single combinational path by ABC because all five pipeline registers are separated by only one logic level each in the unoptimised description), a fanout BUF driving ten loads across the accumulator width, and a final AND2 masking the result with the output-enable predicate before the register captures it; the total combinational delay reported by `abc -stime` is **5.38 ns**, which rises to **5.86 ns** after adding the DFF clock-to-Q arc (0.30 ns) and the destination setup time (0.18 ns), leaving a worst-case slack of **+4.14 ns** against the 10 ns clock target. This path is critical because the INT8 multiply-accumulate tree is the deepest combinational cone in the design — nine 8×8 signed multiplications feed a balanced adder tree whose carry propagation cannot be parallelised without restructuring the arithmetic — and two approaches would shorten it: replacing the implicit ripple-carry accumulation with an explicitly instantiated **carry-save adder (CSA) tree** that converts the nine 16-bit products into two redundant-form partial sums before the final carry-propagate addition, which would reduce the adder depth from roughly 20 to about 10 logic levels and bring the path below 4 ns; or **re-pipelining Stage 4** by splitting the final add and quantisation into two half-clocked stages, which would halve the combinational depth at the cost of one additional cycle of pipeline latency.
